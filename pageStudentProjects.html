City of Cantilever

 The prompt for our Cantilever project was for our engineers to revive a city that had been destroyed by a recent hurricane. Mayor Morse, decided to contract the engineers to implement his cantilever cityscape platform he advocated for during his most recent campaign. The engineers were given a $45 dollar budget per by FEMA to build either the most creatvie, strongest, best value, or longest cantilever structure. The students were given a list of materials and machines they could purchase to build their cantilevers. In order to have enough money to redesign their projects, students were advised to use leave 1/3 of their budget for at least a second itteration. We introduced Maker Moments and Stuck Station to our students, two tools from an MIT research project. During the three day project, I observed students taking risks by designing in Adobe Illustrator and TinkerCAD even when they expressed their discomfort with designing in the programs.
